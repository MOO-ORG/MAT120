
# 5. Differentiate

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[\sqrt{x^3+5}\right]$$

### Step 1: Rewrite the square root

$$\sqrt{x^3+5}=(x^3+5)^{1/2}$$

### Step 2: Use the chain rule

If

$$\frac{d}{dx}[u^{1/2}]  
=\frac12u^{-1/2}\frac{du}{dx},$$

where

$$u=x^3+5.$$

Then

$$\frac{du}{dx}=3x^2.$$

Therefore,

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[(x^3+5)^{1/2}\right]  
=\frac12(x^3+5)^{-1/2}(3x^2).$$

Hence,

$$\boxed{\frac{3x^2}{2\sqrt{x^3+5}}}$$

---

# 6. Differentiate

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{x}{x^2+3}\right]$$

Use the quotient rule:

$$\left(\frac{u}{v}\right)'  
=\frac{u'v-uv'}{v^2}.$$

Let

$$u=x,\qquad v=x^2+3.$$

Then

$$u'=1,\qquad v'=2x.$$

Substituting:

$$=\frac{(1)(x^2+3)-x(2x)}{(x^2+3)^2}$$ $$=\frac{x^2+3-2x^2}{(x^2+3)^2}$$ $$=\boxed{\frac{3-x^2}{(x^2+3)^2}}.$$

---

# 7. Differentiate

$$\frac{d}{dx}[\sin(2\sqrt{x})]$$

Use the chain rule.

Let

$$u=2\sqrt{x}=2x^{1/2}.$$

Then

$$u'=2\left(\frac12x^{-1/2}\right)=x^{-1/2}  
=\frac1{\sqrt{x}}.$$

Since

$$\frac{d}{dx}(\sin u)=\cos u\cdot u',$$

we get

$$=\cos(2\sqrt{x})\cdot\frac1{\sqrt{x}}.$$

Therefore,

$$\boxed{\frac{\cos(2\sqrt{x})}{\sqrt{x}}}.$$

---

# 8. Differentiate

$$\frac{d}{dx}[\sin x-x\cos x]$$

Differentiate term by term.

### First term

$$\frac{d}{dx}(\sin x)=\cos x.$$

### Second term

Use the product rule:

$$\frac{d}{dx}(x\cos x)  
=(1)(\cos x)+x(-\sin x).$$

Thus

$$=\cos x-x\sin x.$$

Since the original expression is

$$\sin x-x\cos x,$$

its derivative is

$$\cos x-(\cos x-x\sin x).$$

Simplifying,

$$\boxed{x\sin x}.$$

$$a=1,$$

$$\int\frac{dx}{x\sqrt{x^2-1}}

$$x^3+x+1$$

$$\frac{x^3+x+1}{x^2+1}

Thus,

$$\int\left(x+\frac1{x^2+1}\right)dx.$$

Integrating,

$$\frac{x^2}{2}+\tan^{-1}x.$$
